Digging through the Digital

Human behavior has always interested me. Following this passion in college, I began focusing on the computational study of neuroscience, exploring the quantitative data behind human behavior. However, my career took a turn once I started delving into the world of technology. Here I found the very culprit that completely altered human behavior on all levels, including the ways we communicate with one another, consume entertainment, and live out our daily lives. Now I find myself as an 'explorer' of technology and the digital world. What has been the affect of mobile technology on people's lives? What defines a pleasant user-experience? How has our interaction with technology changed over time? I currently find myself at the intersection of technology and media, where the digital disruption is rampant, if not the very force that controls media itself. Every day I get my hands dirty in the digital.

Emotions - Can You Read Me Now?

Psychology claims that there exist core emotions that are universally recognized. This work explores four of them:

joy,surprise, sadness, and anger

The images investigate the universality of emotions as they transcend cultural and demographic bounds. Four pieces of the work are grids representing these four emotions performed by a group of people. Unlike the others, the fifth piece questions the homogeneity and manifestation of emotion itself. When asked to portray the emotion that they genuinely felt that day, subjects presented emotions that may or may not have been so superficially visible. They may even display a visage that cannot be understood.
